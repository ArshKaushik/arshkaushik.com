## 3. Four lenses

### 3.1 Recruiter / talent partner — **Verdict: BORDERLINE**

The 30–90s scan, simulated honestly:

- **Would you surface in a "Senior Product Designer" search?** Partially. Headline ("Product Designer & Engineer | Enterprise & Consumer UX | Design + Strategy + Code | Agentic Design") is keyword-rich for the 2026 differentiators — "Agentic Design," "Enterprise UX," and Top Skills "Spec-Driven Development / Enterprise UX / AI Design" are exactly what AI-forward sourcers filter on. But "Senior" appears nowhere, and many recruiter queries filter on title keywords + years; your visible current role is a grad-school consultancy.
- **Is your level legible?** No — and this is the core problem. The scan shows: current student (grad 2027) → five roles in 2021–22 labeled Product Designer (which the resume calls internships) → 2.5 years of full-time core. A rubric-driven recruiter at a scale-up ("senior = 5+ years post-internship") screens this out before reading a single bullet. An AI-native startup recruiter reads "built an MCP for design-system governance" and books the call. Your funnel outcome depends almost entirely on *which recruiter*.
- **Red flags they'd catch:** the intern-title mismatch (table above, #1) and UN-as-employer (#2) are exactly what sourcers cross-check; zero recommendations visible in the export (if the real profile has none, that's missing social proof at the moment it matters most); the About typo.
- **Would they forward you?** For senior req's at rubric-driven orgs: pass on YoE. For mid-level product designer, design engineer, or founding-designer searches: yes — the AI signal is strong enough to forward with a note.

**Because:** recruiters don't evaluate judgment, they evaluate legibility and risk — and this package is illegible at the seniority layer and carries three checkable inconsistencies.

## 4. The 2026 senior-PD bar, and where you sit against it

The post-2023 correction never fully reversed: fewer pure-craft senior seats, rubrics weighting **measurable business impact**, **systems thinking**, **AI/tooling fluency**, and **narrative clarity**; and a structural rise of the **design-engineer hybrid**, which AI-assisted development turned from novelty into a hiring category. Against that:

- **AI fluency:** the market's table stakes are now "uses AI in the workflow." The differentiator tier is *builds the tooling* — your custom MCP for design-system governance, spec-driven development, agentic production shipping. **You are in the differentiator tier**, and it's documented in artifacts (case studies, the "Experience Engineer" title, this codebase), not just claimed. Almost nothing else in your package is rare; this is.
- **Systems:** org-scale design-system transition co-led — at bar and current (code-first systems are the 2026 conversation).
- **Measurable impact:** present everywhere, uneven in rigor (§5, dimension 3).
- **Storytelling:** portfolio narrative is above bar; the cross-artifact story (resume/LinkedIn) is below it (§2).
- **Where you skew:** stronger for **early-stage/AI-forward** (they buy capability and velocity), weaker for **big-tech ladders** (they buy tenure, leveled scope, and led-through-others evidence you can't yet show at senior volume).

## 6. Seniority synthesis

**The package reads: emerging senior — senior-grade judgment, range, and tooling fluency on a mid-level tenure and leadership base.**

- **What reads senior:** the judgment quality of the hardest calls; operating at org scope (changing how 30 designers work, not just shipping screens); cross-functional influence with receipts (convincing PM/eng off table patterns; getting UX analytics onto a roadmap); intellectual honesty in impact claims.
- **What reads mid:** 2.5 years of full-time core with a 17-month max tenure; leadership evidence tops out at co-managing interns and mentoring-through-pairing — there is no evidence yet of setting direction for other senior designers, owning hiring/quality bars, or sustained influence across quarters, which is the mid→senior separator big orgs actually test; and the current full-time-student status caps the "operating at level right now" claim.

**Net:** junior — no. Mid — underselling the judgment. Senior — defensible at AI-forward startups today, not yet defensible on a big-tech leveling call. Staff-leaning — only in dimension 6, and one dimension doesn't level a candidate.
